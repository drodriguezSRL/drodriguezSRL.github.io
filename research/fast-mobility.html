Fast Mobility

[Placeholder — adapt from your long biography and CV.] Current planetary rovers move at a walking pace. This research line challenges that assumption: it started in 2018 as the "High-speed lunar locomotion" project, a collaboration between the European Space Agency and Tohoku University, looking at ways to enable lunar rovers to travel at speeds two orders of magnitude higher than those of current missions. The work spans suspension mechanisms capable of withstanding high-speed perturbations on unstructured terrain, terramechanics (how wheels interact with planetary soil at speed), and the control and planning problems that come with moving fast over terrain you can't fully see coming.

[Placeholder] A prototype rover, Explorer 1 (EX1), was built and tested at Tohoku University's Space Robotics Lab to validate a novel mechanically-hybrid suspension design under high-speed conditions.
